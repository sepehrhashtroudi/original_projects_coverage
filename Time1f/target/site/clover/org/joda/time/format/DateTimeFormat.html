32    /**
33    * Factory that creates instances of DateTimeFormatter from patterns and styles.
34    * <p>
35    * Datetime formatting is performed by the {@link DateTimeFormatter} class.
36    * Three classes provide factory methods to create formatters, and this is one.
37    * The others are {@link ISODateTimeFormat} and {@link DateTimeFormatterBuilder}.
38    * <p>
39    * This class provides two types of factory:
40    * <ul>
41    * <li>{@link #forPattern(String) Pattern} provides a DateTimeFormatter based on
42    * a pattern string that is mostly compatible with the JDK date patterns.
43    * <li>{@link #forStyle(String) Style} provides a DateTimeFormatter based on a
44    * two character style, representing short, medium, long and full.
45    * </ul>
46    * <p>
47    * For example, to use a patterm:
48    * <pre>
49    * DateTime dt = new DateTime();
50    * DateTimeFormatter fmt = DateTimeFormat.forPattern("MMMM, yyyy");
51    * String str = fmt.print(dt);
52    * </pre>
53    *
54    * The pattern syntax is mostly compatible with java.text.SimpleDateFormat -
55    * time zone names cannot be parsed and a few more symbols are supported.
56    * All ASCII letters are reserved as pattern letters, which are defined as follows:
57    * <blockquote>
58    * <pre>
59    * Symbol Meaning Presentation Examples
60    * ------ ------- ------------ -------
61    * G era text AD
62    * C century of era (&gt;=0) number 20
63    * Y year of era (&gt;=0) year 1996
64    *
65    * x weekyear year 1996
66    * w week of weekyear number 27
67    * e day of week number 2
68    * E day of week text Tuesday; Tue
69    *
70    * y year year 1996
71    * D day of year number 189
72    * M month of year month July; Jul; 07
73    * d day of month number 10
74    *
75    * a halfday of day text PM
76    * K hour of halfday (0~11) number 0
77    * h clockhour of halfday (1~12) number 12
78    *
79    * H hour of day (0~23) number 0
80    * k clockhour of day (1~24) number 24
81    * m minute of hour number 30
82    * s second of minute number 55
83    * S fraction of second millis 978
84    *
85    * z time zone text Pacific Standard Time; PST
86    * Z time zone offset/id zone -0800; -08:00; America/Los_Angeles
87    *
88    * ' escape for text delimiter
89    * '' single quote literal '
90    * </pre>
91    * </blockquote>
92    * The count of pattern letters determine the format.
93    * <p>
94    * <strong>Text</strong>: If the number of pattern letters is 4 or more,
95    * the full form is used; otherwise a short or abbreviated form is used if
96    * available.
97    * <p>
98    * <strong>Number</strong>: The minimum number of digits.
99    * Shorter numbers are zero-padded to this amount.
100    * When parsing, any number of digits are accepted.
101    * <p>
102    * <strong>Year</strong>: Numeric presentation for year and weekyear fields
103    * are handled specially. For example, if the count of 'y' is 2, the year
104    * will be displayed as the zero-based year of the century, which is two
105    * digits.
106    * <p>
107    * <strong>Month</strong>: 3 or over, use text, otherwise use number.
108    * <p>
109    * <strong>Millis</strong>: The exact number of fractional digits.
110    * If more millisecond digits are available then specified the number will be truncated,
111    * if there are fewer than specified then the number will be zero-padded to the right.
112    * When parsing, only the exact number of digits are accepted.
113    * <p>
114    * <strong>Zone</strong>: 'Z' outputs offset without a colon, 'ZZ' outputs
115    * the offset with a colon, 'ZZZ' or more outputs the zone id.
116    * <p>
117    * <strong>Zone names</strong>: Time zone names ('z') cannot be parsed.
118    * <p>
119    * Any characters in the pattern that are not in the ranges of ['a'..'z']
120    * and ['A'..'Z'] will be treated as quoted text. For instance, characters
121    * like ':', '.', ' ', '#' and '?' will appear in the resulting time text
122    * even they are not embraced within single quotes.
123    * <p>
124    * DateTimeFormat is thread-safe and immutable, and the formatters it returns
125    * are as well.
126    *
127    * @author Brian S O'Neill
128    * @author Maxim Zhao
129    * @since 1.0
130    * @see ISODateTimeFormat
131    * @see DateTimeFormatterBuilder
132    */
